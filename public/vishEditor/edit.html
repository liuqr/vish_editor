        1. Welcome to ViSH Editor!

          ViSH Editor is an innovative web application that allows you to create amazing web slideshows and novel educational resources such as Virtual Excursions.

          To learn how to use ViSH Editor you can watch several video tutorials in our YouTube Channel. These tutorials cover from basic usage for beginners to advanced features.

          The following video tutorial will teach you how to getting started with ViSH Editor in a few minutes.

          Getting Started with ViSH Editor

           

          If you prefer you can click here to see a text tutorial which will teach you quickly the basics to get started.

        1. Welcome to the Settings screen

          Here you can specify information about your Virtual Excursion.

          The title and avatar (an image to identify your excursion in the ViSH platform) are mandatory fields, so you should fill them before publish your excursion.

          At the left side, you can see a preview of how your excursion will be presented in the ViSH Platform.

           

        2. Write a title (Mandatory)

          Type the title of your excursion in this input. The title is important because it is the one that will appear in the ViSH Platform and the one that other users of the platform will see.

        3. Add an avatar for your excursion (Mandatory)

          Click on this button to choose an image to represent your excursion. This image will be the avatar of the excursion and is important because it will be shown in the ViSH Platform, allowing users to identify the excursion.

        1. Welcome to the Pedagogical Aspects screen

          In this screen you can specify more pedagogy related information (or metadata) about your excursion. These metadata are grounded on and are compliant with the Learning Object Metadata (LOM) standard. The last specification of this standard, which explains in a detailed way all fields of this screen, can be found here.

           

          Below is given a brief explanation of each of the fields that can be specified here.

           

          Language: The language of your excursion.

          Context: The principal environment within which the learning and use of this learning object (i.e. the excursion) is intended to take place.

          Age range: Age of the typical intended user.

          Difficulty: How hard it is to work with or through this learning object.

          Typical Learning Time: Approximate or typical time it takes to work with this learning object.

          Subject: The topic (or topics) of the excursion.

          Educational Objectives: Comments on how this learning object is to be used.

        1. Smartcards

          Smartcards are interactive elements that can be inserted in the presentation in the same way that common slides do.

          The following type of Smartcards are available:

          Flashcard

          Flashcards are resources presented as an image background with 'hot zones' identified by arrows on which the user can click to see additional contents that had been previously tagged. Click here to learn how to create a Flashcard.

          Virtual Tour

          Virtual Tours are similar to Flashcards, but instead of being presented as static background images, they are presented as interactive maps where the 'hot zones' are places identified by map pins. This way, when users click on a map pin (a tagged location), they will see additional contents. Click here to learn how to create a Virtual Tour.

          Enriched Video

          Enriched Videos are videos augmented with other resources (e.g. quizzes, slides, images, etc.), which can be selected and customized by the author, and that can be displayed automatically when the video reaches a specific point of time or explicitly when the user select one of them on a side menu. Click here to learn how to create a Enriched Video.

        1. Creating Virtual Tours

          Virtual Tours are similar to Flashcards, but instead of being presented as static background images, they are presented as interactive maps where the 'hot zones' are places identified by map pins. This way, when users click on a map pin (a tagged location), they will see additional contents.

          To start creating a Virtual Tour click on the 'Add slide' button at the left and then click on the Virtual Tour icon in the 'Smartcards' panel. After that, an empty Virtual Tour will be shown in the screen.

          Virtual Tour Icon

          When you are creating a Virtual Tour, an interactive map is displayed with a search box on its top. You can use this box to look for places and locations. Type the location you want to search, for example Madrid. A list of results will be shown at the bottom of the search box. You can pick any of them or just press the 'enter' key to automatically select the first one. After this, the map will show the selected location.

          When you are creating a Virtual Tour, a new panel is displayed at the bottom of the screen. We will refer to this panel as the 'Virtual Tour panel'.

           

          To add a new slide to the Virtual Tour click on the 'Add slide' button placed in the 'Virtual Tour panel' and continue with the usual process. After add it, the thumbnail of the new slide will appear in the panel and the slide will be displayed in the screen.

           

          When a slide is selected, is displayed in the screen and you can edit and remove it the same way as you do with common slides. To select a slide just click on it in the 'Virtual Tour panel'. If you want to select the Virtual Tour again to edit it, you have to click on the Virtual Tour icon placed at the left of the 'Virtual Tour panel'.

          The slides have draggable arrows on their top. You can drag any of them into the map to create a new location. Once you dragged an arrow into the map, a new map pin will appear in the selected location. This way, when users click on this map pin they will see the associated slide. The map pins are also draggables, so you can drag them across the map. To remove a map pin, just click on it.

          Remember that you can preview the object you are creating at any time by clicking on the 'Preview' button placed in the toolbar .

           

        1. Creating Enriched Videos

          Enriched Videos are videos augmented with other resources (e.g. quizzes, slides, images, etc.), which can be selected and customized by the author, and that can be displayed automatically when the video reaches a specific point of time or explicitly when the user select one of them on a side menu.

          Enriched Videos are designed with MOOCs (Massive Open Online Courses) in mind, with the aim of integrating questions and quizzes in video lectures as well as additional contents. However, their flexibility allows them to be used in many other educational environments.

           

          To start creating an Enriched Video click on the 'Add slide' button at the left and then click on the Enriched Video icon in the 'Smartcards' panel. After that, an empty Enriched Video will be shown in the screen.

          Enriched Video Icon

           

          First off, you should select the video source of the Enriched Video by clicking on the following button:

          You can change the selected video source at any time by clicking on the 'Video' button of the toolbar.

          You can use YouTube and HTML5 videos (mp4, webm and ogg formats). Also, you can upload your own video to ViSH to be converted to HTML5 compliant formats ensuring its full compatibility. Click here to learn more about videos in ViSH Editor.

          When you are creating an Enriched Video, two main elements are displayed on the screen: the video player placed at the left side, and the 'Table of Contents' placed at the right side. The video player allows to play the video the same way classic media players do. The 'Table of Contents' allows to add 'Timestamp Links'. A timestamp link allows viewers to jump to a specific point in the video. The timestamp links are represented by lines displayed in the video progress bar and their titles are displayed in the table of contents. To edit or remove a timestamp link you can use the icons placed at its side in the table of contents.

          You can also create more advanced timestamp links, linking not only to a specific point in time, but also to a slide that contains additional resources. In this case, the slide will be displayed automatically when the video reaches the timestamp or when the user click on the corresponding title in the table of contents. This type of links are represented by balls (instead of simple lines) displayed in the video progress bar. The following steps explain how to create these links.

          When you are creating an Enriched Video, a new panel is displayed at the bottom of the screen. We will refer to this panel as the 'Enriched Video panel'.

           

          To add a new slide to the Enriched Video click on the 'Add slide' button placed in the 'Enriched Video panel' and continue with the usual process. After add it, the thumbnail of the new slide will appear in the panel and the slide will be displayed in the screen.

           

          When a slide is selected, is displayed in the screen and you can edit and remove it the same way as you do with common slides. To select a slide just click on it in the 'Enriched Video panel'. If you want to select the Enriched Video again to edit it, you have to click on the Enriched Video icon placed at the left of the 'Enriched Video panel'.

          The slides have draggable arrows on their top. You can drag any of them to any area of the Enriched Video to create a new timestamp link associated with its slide. This link will be automatically added to the video and the 'Table of Contents'. The link is represented in the video as a ball with the letter of its associated slide inside. You can slide this ball along the video progress bar to adjust the timestamp. You can also edit the timestamp and the title of the link through the icons placed at its side in the table of contents. If a slide of the Enriched Video is deleted, the associated timestamp link (and the ball) will be also deleted.

          You can also add several slides to the same point of time. This is very useful to include large quizzes split in several slides or to combine different type of resources. To do that, just set the same timestamp to all links that are associated with the slides you want to display together. After that, a couple of arrows will be displayed in the table of contents at the side of these links. You can use these arrows to specify the order in which the contents will be displayed when the video reaches that timestamp.

           

          Remember that you can preview the object you are creating at any time by clicking on the 'Preview' button placed in the toolbar .

        1. Uploading videos

          You can upload your own video from your computer. To do that, go to http://vishub.org and upload your video file to the ViSH repository. Several video formats (mp4, flv, mpg, avi, ...) are allowed. When you finish uploading the video onto ViSH, it will be converted to all HTML5 compatible video formats (mp4, webm and ogg). Note that this process could take several minutes or even hours depending on the video size. This is a good way to insert the videos because thereby they will be available in several formats and will work in all HTML5 compliant web browsers. Once the process finish, you have two ways of adding the video:

          • Search your video on the ViSH repository from the ViSH Editor through the 'ViSH' tab. In this case you will have to wait several hours (maximum) for the ViSH search engine to index the new video.
          •  
          • Another way is to go to the page of the video in ViSH (for example, http://vishub.org/videos/3366) and click on the 'embed' tab. After that, you should paste the provided embed code into the input text field of the 'url' tab in the ViSH Editor. This way, the video will be inserted immediately.

           

          Since ViSH Editor allows to embed YouTube videos, you can also upload your videos to YouTube: http://www.youtube.com/upload. Once the video has been successfully uploaded to YouTube, you can insert it by its url, the embed code provided by YouTube, or by searching it from the ViSH Editor through the 'YouTube' tab. YouTube also provide the videos in HTML5 compliant video formats, so they will work in nearly all devices including mobile phones.
